About

Connecting individuals involved in Trap-Neuter-Return(TNR) enabling them access and offer essential services.

Trapperaid is a platform that centralizes services (funds, holding space, transport) while providing the local governments an access to the data about the numbers of spayed/neutered cats, their treatment cost, and veterinary offices that offer discounted services to feral cats.

Every person willing to help feral cats can join Trapperaid!

AFTER BECOMING A MEMBER YOU WILL BE ABLE TO

Raise funds to spay/neuter your colony cats.
Find and offer trapping services, holding space, and transportation.
Schedule and pay for veterinary appointments at the clinic.
Why We Do It

Shelters and rescues are stacked and unable to control the rapid spread of cat overpopulation while local governments do not have enough data to understand where to allocate the resources that could help to tackle the problem.

CAT OVERPOPULATION PROBLEM

500.000 - 1 m feral cats

Flatbush Cats, a nonprofit organization in Brooklyn, estimates that there are as many as half a million feral cats living in New York City. Many other organizations estimate it to be as high as 1 million.

45.5% cat rescuers

According to a survey conducted by our team, 45.5% of cat rescuers have a major problem in accessing low-cost spay/neuter appts, and 21% of them say they can’t afford to treat sick cats before fixing them.

15% over humane
capacity

The ACC is 15% over its humane capacity for cats. In late July 2023, the ACC reached "critical capacity" and could only accept cat surrenders in emergency situations or if they were a safety risk.

1.3 - 4 billion birds killed each year

According to All About Birds it is estimated that cats kill 1.3–4 billion birds each year in the U.S. alone, with 69% of these kills attributable to feral or unowned cats. 

How Can We Help

Solo cat rescuers, shelters, rescues, local governments, veterinary clinics, and rescue-related cat organizations can benefit from Trapperaid platform.

  1. Cat Rescuers
  2. Shelters/Rescues
  3. Local Governments
  4. Vets Clinics
  5. Large Organizations

Upon registering to Trapperaid, solo cats rescuers can offer and get access to essential resources:

  • Funds for spaying/neutering colony cats and covering their medical treatments.
  • Holding spaces for cats before and after the surgery.
  • Transport for cats to veterinary appointments, and pick up/drop off at the Trapper’s convenience.
  • Help in trapping colony cats.

Trapperaid can reduce the cat population and take burden from shelters/rescues that can’t accept more cats.

  • Resource allocation: Trapperaid will provide list of available traps, transport options, and holding spaces, streamlining collaboration among rescues, TNR-certified individuals, and vetted volunteers.
  • Service Scheduling: Use the platform to schedule TNR services at vet clinics and coordinate with volunteers for trapping, transport, and aftercare.
  • Data Tracking: Record when, where, and what TNR services are being used, and by whom (rescue or TNR-certified individual), to quantify rescue allocation and support funding allocation.

Trapperaid can deliver data to local governments that would be able to understand where and how to allocate the resources.

  • Data-Driven Decision Making: Use the platform’s centralized data to make informed decisions on resource allocation, public health strategies, and budget planning.
  • Monitoring and Reporting: Access regular reports that outline TNR activities, costs, and impact to ensure city resources are utilized efficiently
  • Collaboration and Partnerships: Work with TNR organizations, vet clinics, and community members to enhance TNR efforts and address the feral cat population challenge holistically.

Register to Trapperaid as a vet clinic, faciliate your scheduling process with us, and get potential tax breaks.

  • Vets can register their clinics and services on the platform, listing the discounted TNR services they provide.
  • Vets will receive service requests directly through the platform, streamlining the process of scheduling appointments for TNR-certified individuals and rescues.
  • Potential tax incentives or subsidies for vet clinicts that join the standardized pricing model, thus expanding the availability of discounted TNR services.
